# Vehicle Lifecycle Management

## Acquisition Process

New vehicle acquisitions follow a structured approval and procurement process:

1. **Needs Assessment**: The requesting department submits a Vehicle Acquisition Request identifying the operational need, proposed vehicle class, estimated annual mileage, and number of operators. The Fleet Manager reviews the request against current fleet utilization data to determine if the need can be met by reassigning an existing underutilized vehicle.

2. **Budget Approval**: If a new acquisition is justified, the Fleet Manager prepares a Total Cost of Ownership (TCO) estimate covering purchase price, projected fuel costs, insurance, maintenance, and disposal value over the expected service life. The estimate is submitted to the Finance Department for budget approval.

3. **Specification Development**: The Fleet Manager develops vehicle specifications based on the operational need, including minimum payload/towing capacity, fuel type preference, required equipment and upfitting, and any regulatory requirements (emissions, CDL class). Specifications must prioritize standardization with existing fleet vehicles to reduce parts inventory and training costs.

4. **Procurement**: Vehicles are procured through the municipal purchasing process, typically via state contract pricing or competitive bid for specialized equipment. Delivery timelines average 8 to 16 weeks for standard vehicles and up to 12 months for specialized or heavy equipment.

## Vehicle Replacement Criteria

Vehicles are evaluated for replacement based on age, mileage or hours, and maintenance cost trends. A vehicle meeting any one of the following thresholds is flagged for replacement review:

- **Sedans and passenger vehicles**: 100,000 miles or 8 years of service, whichever comes first.
- **Light trucks and vans (under 10,000 lbs GVWR)**: 120,000 miles or 10 years.
- **Medium and heavy trucks (over 10,000 lbs GVWR)**: 150,000 miles or 12 years.
- **Heavy equipment (loaders, backhoes, graders)**: 8,000 operating hours or 15 years.
- **Emergency and specialty vehicles**: Evaluated individually based on condition and regulatory compliance.

Additionally, any vehicle whose annual maintenance cost exceeds 50% of its current replacement value should be prioritized for replacement regardless of age or mileage.

Reaching a replacement threshold does not automatically trigger replacement. The Fleet Manager conducts a condition assessment considering: overall mechanical condition, body and frame condition, reliability history (unscheduled downtime), operator feedback, and availability of replacement parts.

## Fleet Right-Sizing

An annual fleet utilization review is conducted each January to identify underutilized vehicles. Vehicles averaging fewer than 500 miles per month (or 40 hours per month for equipment) are flagged for reassignment or disposal review.

The review process includes: pulling 12-month utilization data from the FleetWise system, interviewing assigned departments about operational needs, identifying pooling opportunities (shared vehicles for low-utilization departments), and recommending disposals, reassignments, or pool conversions.

## Disposal Process

Vehicles approved for disposal follow this process:

1. **Surplus Declaration**: The Fleet Manager completes a Surplus Vehicle Declaration form listing the vehicle's asset number, VIN, condition rating, estimated fair market value, and reason for disposal.

2. **Data and Branding Removal**: All municipal branding (decals, logos, paint schemes) must be removed. GPS units, radios, and any other municipal equipment must be recovered. License plates are returned to the licensing authority.

3. **Auction or Sale**: Surplus vehicles are sold through the municipal auction process, typically conducted quarterly. Vehicles may also be sold through online government surplus auction platforms. Minimum bid prices are set at 10% of original acquisition cost or current fair market value, whichever is higher.

4. **Record Closure**: Upon sale, the vehicle record in FleetWise is updated with the disposal date, sale price, and buyer information. The asset is marked as "Disposed" and removed from the active fleet roster.

## Electric Vehicle Transition Plan

The municipality has committed to transitioning 30% of light-duty fleet vehicles to electric or plug-in hybrid by 2030. Priority for EV replacement is given to vehicles with predictable daily routes under 150 miles, access to overnight charging at their home facility, and sedans or light trucks that can be replaced with currently available EV models. Charging infrastructure installation is coordinated with the Facilities Department and budgeted separately from vehicle acquisition.
