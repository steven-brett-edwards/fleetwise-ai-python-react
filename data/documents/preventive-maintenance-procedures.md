# Preventive Maintenance Procedures

## Oil and Fluid Service Intervals

All fleet vehicles must follow manufacturer-recommended oil change intervals adjusted for municipal duty cycles. Gasoline vehicles require oil and filter changes every 5,000 miles or 6 months, whichever comes first. Diesel vehicles require oil and filter changes every 7,500 miles or 6 months. CNG (compressed natural gas) vehicles follow the same 5,000-mile interval as gasoline but require CNG-specific oil formulations rated for natural gas engines.

Transmission fluid should be inspected every 30,000 miles and replaced every 60,000 miles. Coolant must be tested at every oil change and replaced every 50,000 miles or 3 years. Power steering fluid should be checked monthly and replaced if contaminated or degraded.

## Tire Maintenance

Tire rotation is required every 7,500 miles for all vehicles. Tire pressure must be checked weekly by the assigned operator using a calibrated gauge. Tires must be replaced when tread depth reaches 4/32 of an inch for passenger vehicles and light trucks, or 6/32 of an inch for heavy-duty vehicles and those regularly operating in wet conditions.

Wheel alignment should be checked every 12 months or after any impact event (pothole, curb strike). Vehicles pulling to one side, exhibiting uneven tire wear, or with steering wheel vibration should be submitted for immediate alignment inspection.

## Brake System Inspection

Brake inspections are required every 15,000 miles or 12 months. Brake pad thickness must be measured; pads below 3mm require immediate replacement. Brake rotors must be inspected for scoring, warping, or thickness below manufacturer minimum specifications.

Brake fluid must be tested for moisture content every 12 months and replaced every 2 years or 50,000 miles. Vehicles exhibiting soft brake pedal feel, unusual noise, or increased stopping distance must be taken out of service immediately and submitted for emergency brake inspection.

## Filter Replacement Schedule

Engine air filters must be replaced every 15,000 miles or annually. Cabin air filters must be replaced every 12,000 miles or annually. Fuel filters on diesel vehicles must be replaced every 20,000 miles. Fuel filters on gasoline vehicles are typically lifetime components but should be inspected if fuel delivery issues arise.

## CDL Vehicle Pre-Trip and Post-Trip Inspections

All vehicles requiring a Commercial Driver's License (CDL) must undergo a documented pre-trip inspection before each shift and a post-trip inspection at the end of each shift. The pre-trip inspection must cover: engine compartment (fluid levels, belts, hoses, leaks), cab interior (gauges, controls, mirrors, seatbelt), exterior walk-around (tires, lights, body damage, coupling devices), and brake system (air pressure buildup, brake test).

Any defect identified during pre-trip inspection must be reported on the Vehicle Condition Report (VCR) form. Critical defects — including brake system failures, tire blowouts, steering defects, or lighting failures — require the vehicle to be taken out of service until repaired.

## Seasonal Maintenance

### Winterization (October 1 - November 15)
All vehicles must complete winterization before November 15. This includes: coolant antifreeze protection verified to -30F, battery load test and terminal cleaning, wiper blade replacement, washer fluid topped with winter-grade solution, tire chains inspected and stored in vehicle (where applicable), and emergency kit verification (blanket, flashlight, flares).

### Spring/Summer Preparation (March 15 - April 30)
Spring preparation includes: A/C system performance test and refrigerant level check, coolant system pressure test, removal and storage of tire chains, and general underbody inspection for salt/corrosion damage from winter operations.
