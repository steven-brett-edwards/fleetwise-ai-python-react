# Fleet Safety Policies

## Pre-Trip Vehicle Inspection

Every operator must perform a visual pre-trip inspection before operating any fleet vehicle. The inspection covers: all exterior lights and signals are functional, tires are properly inflated with no visible damage, all mirrors are clean and properly adjusted, windshield and windows are free of cracks or obstructions, fluid levels are within acceptable range (visible leaks must be reported), seat belts are functional, and the horn operates correctly.

Any deficiency must be reported to the Fleet Manager before operating the vehicle. Vehicles with safety-critical deficiencies (brakes, steering, tires, lights) must not be operated until repaired.

## Accident and Incident Reporting

All accidents and incidents involving fleet vehicles must be reported to the Fleet Manager within 1 hour of occurrence, regardless of severity. The operator must:

1. Ensure the safety of all persons involved and call emergency services if needed.
2. Do not admit fault or make statements about liability at the scene.
3. Exchange insurance and contact information with other parties.
4. Photograph all damage to all vehicles involved, the scene, and any relevant road conditions or signage.
5. Complete the Fleet Accident Report Form within 24 hours and submit to the Fleet Manager.
6. Submit to a post-accident drug and alcohol screening within 2 hours if the accident involved injury, fatality, or the operator received a citation.

Failure to report an accident within the required timeframe may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination and personal liability for damages.

## Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

PPE requirements vary by vehicle class and duty assignment:

- **Passenger vehicles and light trucks**: No special PPE required beyond seatbelt use.
- **Medium and heavy-duty trucks**: Steel-toed boots, high-visibility vest when outside the cab in work zones or loading areas.
- **Maintenance and repair personnel**: Safety glasses, steel-toed boots, nitrile gloves when handling fluids, hearing protection when operating pneumatic tools or in areas exceeding 85 dB.
- **Fuel handling**: Nitrile gloves and safety glasses required when fueling. No smoking within 50 feet of fueling areas. Anti-static grounding procedures must be followed for bulk fuel transfers.

## Maximum Driving Hours

No operator shall drive a fleet vehicle for more than 10 hours in any consecutive 24-hour period. Operators on trips exceeding 4 hours of continuous driving must take a minimum 30-minute rest break. These limits apply to all fleet vehicles regardless of CDL requirements.

Supervisors are responsible for scheduling that complies with driving hour limits. Operators who feel fatigued must report to their supervisor and cease driving immediately — fatigue-related operation is a terminable offense.

## Distracted Driving and Cell Phone Policy

The use of handheld mobile devices while operating a fleet vehicle is strictly prohibited. This includes making or receiving calls, texting, emailing, using social media, or any other handheld device interaction while the vehicle is in motion or temporarily stopped in traffic.

Hands-free calls are permitted only when the vehicle is equipped with an integrated hands-free system and road conditions allow safe use. Operators must pull over safely and park before engaging in any extended phone conversation.

GPS navigation devices must be programmed before the vehicle is put in motion. Adjusting navigation while driving is prohibited.

## Seatbelt Policy

All occupants of fleet vehicles must wear seat belts at all times when the vehicle is in operation. The vehicle operator is responsible for ensuring all passengers are belted before moving the vehicle. Seatbelt use is mandatory regardless of seating position or trip distance.

## Unauthorized Use and Passengers

Fleet vehicles are for official municipal business only. Personal use of fleet vehicles is prohibited. Unauthorized passengers (non-employees or non-business-related individuals) are not permitted in fleet vehicles without prior written approval from the department director.
