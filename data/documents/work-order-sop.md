# Work Order Standard Operating Procedures

## Submitting a Work Order

Work orders for vehicle repair or maintenance can be submitted through two channels:

1. **FleetWise System**: Log in to the FleetWise application, navigate to the Work Orders section, and select "New Work Order." Provide the vehicle asset number, a description of the issue or requested service, and select the appropriate priority level. The system will auto-assign a work order number in the format WO-YYYY-NNNNN.

2. **Direct Contact**: For urgent or safety-critical issues, contact the Fleet Manager directly by phone or in person. The Fleet Manager will create the work order on your behalf and initiate immediate triage.

All work orders must include: the vehicle asset number, operator name and contact information, a clear description of the symptom or issue (not a diagnosis), when the issue was first noticed, and whether the vehicle is safe to operate.

## Priority Classification

Work orders are classified into four priority levels that determine response time and scheduling:

- **Critical**: Safety risk or vehicle is undrivable. Examples: brake failure, steering loss, engine fire, airbag deployment, fluid leak creating a road hazard. Response time: immediate. Vehicle must be taken out of service and towed to the shop if not drivable.

- **High**: Significant operational impact expected within 24 hours. Examples: check engine light with performance issues, A/C failure during summer heat advisory, transmission slipping, coolant leak (slow). Response time: within 24 hours. Vehicle may remain in limited service at Fleet Manager discretion.

- **Medium**: Issue requires attention but does not affect immediate safety or operability. Examples: minor oil leak, cosmetic damage, non-critical warning lights, interior component malfunction, scheduled maintenance coming due. Response time: scheduled within 1 week.

- **Low**: Convenience or cosmetic items. Examples: paint touch-up, upholstery repair, accessory installation, minor rattle or squeak. Response time: scheduled as shop capacity allows, typically within 30 days.

## Priority Escalation

A work order may be escalated from Medium to High if the condition worsens or if operational needs change. Only the Fleet Manager or Shop Supervisor may escalate priority. Escalation must include a documented justification in the work order notes.

## Parts Ordering and Approval Thresholds

Parts required for work order completion follow a tiered approval process based on cost:

- **Under $500**: Technician may order directly from approved vendors without additional approval.
- **$500 to $2,000**: Requires Fleet Manager approval before ordering. Technician must document the part number, vendor, cost, and justification in the work order.
- **Over $2,000**: Requires Director-level approval. Fleet Manager must submit a Parts Approval Request form with three vendor quotes when available. Emergency exceptions may be granted verbally by the Director with written follow-up within 48 hours.

All parts must be ordered from approved vendors listed in the Vendor Management System. Sole-source parts (OEM-only or single-vendor availability) must be documented with a sole-source justification memo.

## Work Order Completion and Closeout

Upon completing repairs, the assigned technician must:

1. Update the work order with all labor hours, parts used (with part numbers and costs), and a description of work performed.
2. Conduct a quality check — verify the reported issue is resolved and no new issues were introduced.
3. Update the vehicle's mileage or hour meter reading at time of service.
4. Mark the work order status as "Completed" in the system.
5. Notify the requesting operator that the vehicle is ready for pickup.

Work orders remain in "Completed" status for 30 days to allow for warranty claims or follow-up issues, after which they are automatically archived.

## Warranty and Comeback Tracking

If the same issue recurs within 30 days or 1,000 miles of a completed repair, a "Comeback" work order is created referencing the original work order number. Comeback rates are tracked monthly by technician and by repair type. A comeback rate exceeding 5% triggers a review by the Shop Supervisor.
