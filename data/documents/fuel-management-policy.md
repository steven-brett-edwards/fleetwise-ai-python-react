# Fuel Management Policy

## Fuel Card Usage

Each fleet vehicle is assigned a dedicated fuel card linked to its asset number. Fuel cards are assigned to the vehicle, not the operator. The following rules apply to all fuel card transactions:

- Fuel cards may only be used for the vehicle to which they are assigned. Using a fuel card for a different vehicle or personal vehicle is prohibited and may result in disciplinary action.
- Operators must enter the current odometer reading at the pump for each transaction. Failure to enter the odometer reading prevents accurate fuel consumption tracking.
- Receipts must be retained for all fuel purchases and submitted to the Fleet Manager monthly. Digital receipts from the fuel card provider are acceptable.
- Fuel cards must not be used for non-fuel purchases (car washes, food, merchandise) unless specifically authorized by the Fleet Manager for windshield washer fluid or emergency supplies.
- Lost or stolen fuel cards must be reported to the Fleet Manager immediately. The card will be deactivated and a replacement issued within 2 business days.

## Approved Fuel Types

Vehicles must be fueled with the fuel type specified on the fuel card and the vehicle's fuel door label. Using incorrect fuel can cause severe engine damage and will result in the operator being held financially responsible for repairs. The approved fuel types are:

- **Unleaded Regular (87 octane)**: Standard for all gasoline fleet vehicles unless otherwise specified.
- **Diesel (#2 Ultra-Low Sulfur)**: For all diesel fleet vehicles. Premium diesel additives are not authorized without Fleet Manager approval.
- **CNG (Compressed Natural Gas)**: For dedicated CNG vehicles only. CNG fueling is available at the Central Garage fueling station.
- **Electric (Level 2 / DC Fast Charge)**: For battery electric vehicles. Charging is available at designated municipal charging stations.

## CNG Fueling Procedures

CNG vehicles must be fueled exclusively at the Central Garage CNG fueling station. Operators must follow these safety procedures:

1. Park the vehicle at the CNG dispenser and turn off the engine.
2. Set the parking brake and exit the vehicle.
3. Inspect the fueling nozzle and vehicle receptacle for damage or debris.
4. Connect the fueling nozzle securely — you should hear and feel the coupling click into place.
5. Initiate the fueling sequence on the dispenser. Do not leave the fueling area unattended during the fill.
6. When the dispenser indicates the fill is complete, disconnect the nozzle and replace it on the dispenser.
7. Verify no gas odor is present around the receptacle before starting the vehicle.

CNG fueling training is required for all operators assigned to CNG vehicles. Training is provided by the Fleet Division and must be completed before an operator may fuel a CNG vehicle independently.

## Anti-Idling Policy

Fleet vehicles must not idle for more than 3 consecutive minutes except under the following conditions:

- Temperatures below 20F or above 95F when the vehicle is occupied and climate control is necessary for operator safety.
- Emergency or law enforcement operations requiring the vehicle to remain running.
- Vehicles equipped with power take-off (PTO) equipment that must run to operate hydraulic systems, aerial lifts, or similar equipment.
- Diesel vehicles during cold weather warm-up, limited to 5 minutes maximum.

Excessive idling wastes fuel, increases emissions, accelerates engine wear, and increases maintenance costs. Fleet vehicle telematics data is monitored for excessive idle time. Operators with chronic excessive idling will receive coaching from their supervisor.

## Fuel Efficiency Targets

Monthly fuel efficiency is tracked for all vehicles and compared against class-specific targets:

- **Sedans**: 25 MPG minimum average
- **Light trucks and SUVs**: 18 MPG minimum average
- **Vans (cargo and passenger)**: 15 MPG minimum average
- **Medium-duty trucks**: 10 MPG minimum average
- **Heavy-duty trucks**: 6 MPG minimum average
- **CNG vehicles**: Tracked in GGE (gasoline gallon equivalent) with targets matching their gasoline class equivalent.
- **Electric vehicles**: Tracked in kWh per 100 miles. Target: under 35 kWh/100mi for sedans, under 45 kWh/100mi for SUVs and light trucks.

Vehicles consistently performing more than 20% below their class target are flagged for mechanical inspection to identify issues such as tire underinflation, air filter restriction, fuel system problems, or engine performance degradation.

## Monthly Fuel Consumption Reporting

The Fleet Manager generates a monthly fuel consumption report including: total gallons consumed by fuel type, total fuel expenditure, fleet-wide average MPG by vehicle class, top 10 highest fuel consumers, vehicles with unusual consumption patterns (sudden increase may indicate a mechanical issue or misuse), and idle time percentage by vehicle.

This report is distributed to department directors by the 10th of each month for the prior month's data. Departments with vehicles exceeding fuel targets are expected to work with the Fleet Manager to identify corrective actions.
